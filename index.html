Ahmed
Hassan

Ahmed Hassan is an Emmy award-winning filmmaker and cinematographer, best known for his work in the documentary 'The Square' (2014), which followed the Egyptian Revolution of 2011. His work has been supported by various prestigious institutions and festivals, including Sundance, Tribeca, Netflix, Toronto, Berlin, and the International Documentary Association. Ahmed has also worked on various documentaries, short films, commercials, and television shows. He has extensive experience in cinematography, post-production supervision, and filmmaking, showcasing skills in camera setups, lighting, sound, and video editing. His documentary experience is critical, with commercial experience being a desirable asset. He is adept at using editing software such as Final Cut Pro, Adobe Premiere, Adobe Photoshop, and Lightroom. Ahmed is also experienced in investigative video journalism. Ahmed holds a Diploma of Communications from Al Sahafa Technology College in Cairo, Egypt. Some of his notable projects include 'Children of the Revolution', 'Homeland is not a Series', 'Ultras of Egypt', and 'Subject'.
